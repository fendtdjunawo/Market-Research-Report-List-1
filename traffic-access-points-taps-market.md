<p><p>Traffic Access Points (TAPs) serve as crucial interfaces in network monitoring, enabling the capture and analysis of data traffic. The market for TAPs is divided into singlemode and multimode categories. Singlemode TAPs are designed for long-distance communications, using a narrow fiber optic core to minimize signal loss. In contrast, multimode TAPs are suitable for shorter distances, utilizing a wider core to support multiple light paths. Both types cater to different networking requirements, influencing deployment strategies in various environments.</p></p>
