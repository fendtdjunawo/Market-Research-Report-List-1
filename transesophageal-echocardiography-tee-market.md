<p><p>Transesophageal echocardiography (TEE) is a specialized imaging technique used to visualize the heart and surrounding structures via an ultrasound probe inserted into the esophagus. The TEE market includes several types: Two-Dimensional (2D) TEE offers flat images, providing essential heart structure details; Three-Dimensional (3D) TEE enhances visualization by presenting volumetric images; while Four-Dimensional (4D) TEE adds the element of real-time motion, allowing for dynamic assessment of cardiac function. Each type serves specific diagnostic needs in cardiology.</p></p>
