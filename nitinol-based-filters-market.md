<p><p>Nitinol-based filters are medical devices designed to prevent blood clots from traveling from the veins to the lungs. The market is divided into two main types: Permanent IVC filters, which are designed for long-term placement within the inferior vena cava, providing ongoing protection against embolism; and Retrievable IVC filters, which can be removed after the risk of clot formation diminishes. Both types leverage Nitinol's unique properties, such as flexibility and biocompatibility, enhancing patient safety and treatment efficacy.</p></p>
