<p><ul><li>Shunt Reactor</li><li>Series Reactor</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The current limiting reactor market is primarily divided into two types: shunt reactors and series reactors. Shunt reactors are connected parallel to the power system, primarily used for voltage control and reactive power compensation. In contrast, series reactors are placed in line with the electrical circuit, designed to limit short-circuit currents and enhance system stability. Both types play crucial roles in managing electrical flow, ensuring safety, and optimizing the performance of power systems in various applications.</p></p>
